# Blog Post Blueprint

## Structure

- **Teaser question** — A single curiosity-sparking question tied to something the reader uses daily (e.g. Google, Spotify, their phone). Makes the abstract personal before they've read a word.

- **Real-world hook / motivation** — Describe the practical problem in plain language. No math yet. The reader should understand *why this matters* and feel the friction of the unsolved problem.

- **Theory section** — Introduce the mathematical concept that solves the problem. Formal, with LaTeX. But grounded — keep referring back to the real-world example so the reader never loses the thread.

- **Intuition bridge** — A short plain-English paragraph before code: "so what this actually means is...". Prevents the theory → code jump from feeling abrupt.

- **Code demo** — A small, working implementation. Break it into narrative steps rather than one big block. The story continues here.

- **Discussion** — Strengths and weaknesses of the approach. What else can this framework solve? Where does it break down? This is where you show genuine thinking, not just exposition.

- **Summary / conclusion** — Restate the journey: problem → concept → solution. Optional: end with a "so what?" or a teaser toward a related concept.
